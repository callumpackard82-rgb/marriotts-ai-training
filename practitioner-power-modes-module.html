Beyond chat: the power modes

⏱ About 16 minutes🎯 Canvas · Deep Research · Guided Learning🧪 Try one mode
1 Why this matters

So far you've done everything in the chat box — type, scroll, type again. But your tool has a few purpose-built modes that do specific jobs far better than back-and-forth chatting: drafting a document, researching properly, or being tutored step by step.

Knowing they exist — and when to reach for each — is what separates a casual user from a confident one. You'll meet three, and try one for real.

2 The idea

Three modes, three jobs

Plain chat is great for quick exchanges. When the job is bigger, switch mode. All four tools have versions of these — only the names differ.

📝 Canvas / Artifacts / Pages

When: drafting or editing a document

Opens a proper editable document beside the chat. You and the AI work on the same draft — tweak a paragraph, ask for a rewrite of just one section — instead of the whole thing scrolling away each time.

Plain chatWhole letter regenerates every time; you copy-paste to keep up.
CanvasEdit in place, select one line and refine it, watch it evolve.

🔎 Deep Research

When: you need a properly sourced overview

Sends the AI off to read many sources and come back with a longer, cited report — minutes of work instead of an instant guess. Brilliant for "give me the landscape on…", as long as you check the citations.

Plain chatOne quick answer, possibly out of date or invented.
Deep ResearchA structured report with links you can verify.

🎓 Guided Learning

When: you want to learn, not just be told

A tutor mode that walks you through something step by step, checking understanding and asking you questions — rather than dumping the answer. Superb for upskilling yourself, and a model worth knowing about for pupils too.

Plain chat"Here's the answer" — you nod and forget it.
Guided Learning"Let's work it out together" — it sticks.
3 Watch it work

Same task, the wrong way and the right way

Job: write a one-page guide for parents on supporting reading at home.

In plain chat: the AI writes the whole guide; you spot the tone's a bit formal in paragraph two, ask it to redo — and the entire thing regenerates, losing the bits you liked.

In Canvas: the guide opens as an editable document. You highlight just paragraph two and say "warmer, less formal". Only that paragraph changes. You tighten a heading yourself, ask it to add a bulleted "5-minute ideas" box, and you're done in half the time with none of the copy-paste.

That's the pattern for all three modes: the right mode removes the friction the chat box was quietly adding.
4 Your turn

Pick one mode and use it for real

Five minutes. Choose the mode that fits a real job this week and run it:

Canvas — draftingOpen this in a canvas/document. Draft a one-page guide for parents on supporting reading at home with a Year 8 child. Friendly tone, a short intro, five practical tips, and a "5-minute ideas" box. We'll edit it together.
Deep Research — investigatingUse Deep Research. Give me a sourced overview of effective, evidence-based strategies for improving reading fluency at Key Stage 3, with citations I can check.
Guided Learning — upskilling yourselfUse Guided Learning / tutor mode. Teach me how retrieval practice works and how to build it into my lessons. Ask me questions as we go rather than just explaining.

Current as of June 2026
These modes are added, renamed and merged often. If a name here has changed, look for the nearest equivalent — the three jobs (draft a document, research with sources, be tutored) are what endure.
5 🛡️ Data-safety checkpoint

Research mode reads the open web — so check it

Two topic-specific risks here:

  • Deep Research can be wrong or biased. It summarises whatever it finds online — which includes rubbish. Always open the citations and verify before you put anything in front of pupils or parents. Treat it as a well-read starting point, not gospel.
  • Modes don't change the data rule. Don't paste real pupil or staff data into a research prompt or a canvas on a personal account. Anything touching real data goes into Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat with the green shield, and even then — minimise.

Every example prompt above is deliberately generic — no real child needed to learn the mode.
