1 Why this matters

Four big names — Claude, ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot — and a nagging worry that you'll pick the "wrong" one. Good news: for everyday school work they're far more alike than different, and the choice that actually matters isn't about features at all.

By the end you'll know what each one is, the one distinction that does matter in a school, and exactly which tool to open for school work — plus how to confirm it's protecting your data.

2 The idea

They're four cars, not four planets

All four are AI chat assistants: you type, they respond, you refine. They each also offer the same kinds of extra features — a way to save a reusable custom assistant, a document canvas, a research mode, image generation. Only the names differ. Learn the idea once and you can drive any of them.

Claude

Anthropic

Especially strong at writing, reasoning and careful document work. Thoughtful, measured tone.

ChatGPT

OpenAI

The all-rounder most people have heard of. Strong built-in image generation.

Gemini

Google

Sits neatly alongside Google Docs, Slides and Gmail if you live in Workspace.

Copilot ★ School-approved

Microsoft

A protected AI chat you open in Teams, Edge or your browser. Signed in with your school login it has enterprise data protection — so it's our tool for real school work.

The distinction that matters: not "which is cleverest" but "which has a data agreement with our school". For us that's Copilot. The other three are fine for personal use and generic, no-data tasks — but real school data goes in Copilot.

4 🛡️ Data-safety checkpoint

One tool for school data — and how to check it's on

The four tools are not equal when it comes to your pupils' data. The safe-for-school-data version is the one the school has a data agreement for:

For us that's Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat, used signed in with your school account. When you are, prompts and responses stay inside the school's protected Microsoft boundary and aren't used to train the model. You'll see a green shield in the corner confirming enterprise data protection is on. No green shield, or a personal login, = treat it as green-data-only.

Personal Claude, ChatGPT or Gemini accounts have no agreement with the school — perfectly fine for generic tasks with no real data, never for anything identifying a pupil or colleague.

5 Your turn

Find your green shield

Three minutes to set yourself up properly for everything that follows:

  1. Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat — the Copilot icon in Teams or Edge, or copilot.microsoft.com.
  2. Make sure you're signed in with your school account (not a personal Microsoft account).
  3. Look for the green shield / "enterprise data protection" indicator. That's your confirmation you're protected.
  4. Run a simple prompt to get comfortable — copy the one below.
A friendly first promptI'm a teacher trying you out. In plain English, give me three quick ways you could save me time this week, with a one-line example of each.
